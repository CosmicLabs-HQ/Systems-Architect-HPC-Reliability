# Systems Architect, HPC & Reliability

**Location:** In-person, San Francisco (Relocation Supported)
**Type:** Full-time | Competitive Pay + Equity
**Start Date:** ASAP

## What You'll Own

You will own the reliability layer: the failure taxonomies, the telemetry and log signals that matter, and the fault tolerance architecture behind our models. You decide what a failing GPU or a degrading link looks like in the data, and you keep that picture coherent from the silicon up. The work lives low in the stack, close to silicon, firmware, BMC, and the network fabric, not orchestration.

You will:

- Define the failure modes and reliability signals that matter across GPU and CPU fleets: XID errors, silent data corruption, HBM thermal events, NVLink degradation, ECC and memory trends, firmware and BMC behavior
- Correlate device telemetry, firmware and BMC state, and link-level network signals into one coherent picture of fleet health
- Map failure modes to the telemetry and log sources that expose them
- Analyze production telemetry from supercomputers and AI clusters to find the patterns that precede failure
- Design fault tolerance and resilience at the hardware and fabric layer: failure domains, degraded-mode operation, recovery
- Work with design partners to validate failure taxonomies against real incident history

## What You Bring

- 6+ years working close to the hardware in large-scale HPC, AI, or cloud fleets, at the level of silicon, firmware, and the network fabric
- Hands-on experience with device and fabric telemetry (GNMI, Redfish and BMC, NVIDIA DCGM, link-level network signals) used to diagnose or predict hardware faults
- Experience building a prediction, anomaly detection, or reliability analysis system on real operational data, and a clear sense of where those systems break
- Strong grounding in fault tolerance and RAS at the hardware and fabric layer
- The ability to sit between the hardware layer and the modeling layer without losing precision in either
- Strong software engineering habits: version control, reproducible work, testable pipelines

## Bonus

- NVIDIA GPU fleet experience and the failure surface around it: XID and SDC analysis, HBM failure characterization, NVLink behavior
- Low-level networking depth: link-level fabric health, GNMI, large-scale interconnect (InfiniBand, NVLink, Slingshot)
- A telecom or networking background working below the application layer
- Firmware, BMC, or hardware bring-up experience
- Time inside a national lab, hyperscaler, or neocloud reliability or SRE org
- PhD in computer engineering, electrical engineering, computer science, or related
- US person status and the ability to obtain a security clearance, a plus generally and a requirement for certain projects
